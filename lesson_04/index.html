The Beauty of Whittier

1 -- Whittier is surrounded by mountains, waterfalls, and water. Its subarctic climate makes it the wettest city in the United States, with an annual rainfall of 5,024 mm and winds of up to 100 km/h. 51 sq. km is the area of ​​this Alaskan city. It has a population of 214, most of whom live in the same house. We are amazed by the history of the city at the end of the world, and we are eager to tell it to you.
Недалеко від міста розташований льодовик, який у 1915 році назвали на честь поета Джона Грінліф Віттіер (John Greenleaf Whittier). Ми вражені історією міста на краю світу, і нам кортить розповісти її вам.

2 -- During World War II, this area was chosen to organize an ice-free port and military garrison. Whittier was located at some distance from other settlements and transport infrastructure, so a tunnel had to be built. The military facility, port, and railroad formed Camp Sullivan in 1943, which was used until 1960.

3 -- During the Cold War, housing and recreation areas for 1,000 people were built here. In the 1950s, 2 buildings appeared. The first, the Buckner Building, is now in disrepair, and the second, the Begich Towers, is still in use today. Both structures were to become part of a large military complex, and it was planned to build 8 more similar structures for the needs of the army.

4 -- A turning point in the city's history was March 27, 1964 when the Great Alaskan Earthquake occurred. Its magnitude is estimated at 9.2 points, the tremors caused a tsunami. The waves then reached 13 meters and severely damaged the city: several buildings on the waterfront were destroyed.

5 -- The 14-story building was built in 1957 and was originally named for Colonel William Walter Hodge, who commanded the 93rd Engineer Regiment on the Alaska Highway. In 1972, the Begich Towers received their current name in honor of Democratic Congressman Nick Begich, a representative from Alaska. The only building in Whittier has 14 floors and 3 modules, which are interconnected by numerous corridors, elevators and stairs. The building has a flat roof and a rectangular shape. Where does the word “tower” come from in the name, and even in the plural? The fact is that on the north side there are 2 protruding modules that resemble towers.

6 -- There are just over 200 people living in Begić Towers, and the children enter school through a tunnel due to the harsh weather conditions.
That's the entire population of the town. In addition to the apartments, the building houses a post office, a Methodist church, several shops, a laundry, a police station, the city hall, a hospital, and even a small hotel. There are 2 playgrounds in Whittier: one of them is open only in the summer, and the second is under a roof - weather conditions do not always allow young citizens to walk safely outside. On bad days, they go to school through a special tunnel.

7 -- There is no usual division between home and school: children and teachers perceive each other as good neighbors or friends, so there is no need to impose discipline. To stay healthy, Whittier residents go to the school gym, which is open during school hours, but there is no traditional fitness center. Boats can be seen in the parking lot next to the cars.

8 -- The only way to get to Whittier by land is through a tunnel.
The path is blocked by a mountain range, and residents and visitors can travel by plane, boat, train, or car. The latter travel through a tunnel through Mount Maynard. A single-lane road runs through the tunnel, both for cars and trains. A round trip costs $13, while locals use an annual pass worth $500.

9 -- There is a schedule near the tunnel: 15 minutes it allows traffic in one direction, the next 15 minutes - in the other. At night, the road is closed. Locals sometimes go to Anchorage, located about an hour away. However, if you do not have time to return by 10:30 PM, you will have to spend the night in the car.

10 -- Whittier seems like a forgotten city, but tourists come here. After all, cruises start from the port: those who wish can see icebergs and polar bears. Therefore, you can periodically see liners here. The city itself is also of interest - hotel rooms are booked in advance. Locals are engaged in fishing, work in the port, or are associated with tourism.
